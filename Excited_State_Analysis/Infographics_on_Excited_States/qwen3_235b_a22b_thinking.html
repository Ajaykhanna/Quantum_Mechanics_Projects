Why These Methods Matter

TDDFT, TDA, and sTDA are computational techniques used to calculate electronic excitation energies - essential for predicting UV-Vis spectra, photochemical behavior, and optical properties of molecules.

TDA is not a standalone theory - it's an approximation within TDDFT. sTDA is a radically simplified model designed for massive systems.

While all three methods solve the same fundamental problem, they differ dramatically in computational approach, cost, and applicability. This guide breaks down exactly when and why to use each method.

TDDFT

Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory

Core Concept

Solves full time-dependent Kohn-Sham equations using linear response theory. Considers both excitations and de-excitations.

Mathematical Formulation

  • Solves full Casida equation: $$\begin{pmatrix} A & B \\ B^* & A^* \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} X \\ Y \end{pmatrix} = \omega \begin{pmatrix} X \\ -Y \end{pmatrix}$$
  • A = Resonant terms (electron promotion)
  • B = Anti-resonant terms (de-excitations)
  • Non-Hermitian matrix problem (4N² × 4N²)

Best For

Small to medium molecules (up to 100 atoms) where high accuracy is needed for valence excitations.

TDA

Tamm-Dancoff Approximation

Core Concept

Simplified version of TDDFT that ignores de-excitation terms. Not a standalone theory - an approximation within TDDFT.

Mathematical Formulation

  • Sets B = 0 in Casida equation: $$A X = \omega X$$
  • Only considers single excitations (i → a)
  • Hermitian matrix problem (N² × N²)
  • Fixes TDDFT's poor treatment of charge-transfer states

Best For

Medium-sized molecules (100-500 atoms) with charge-transfer excitations or triplet states.

sTDA

simplified Tamm-Dancoff Approximation

Core Concept

Ultra-fast semi-empirical method that replaces quantum integrals with parametrized functions.

Mathematical Formulation

  • No explicit DFT calculation - uses precomputed parameters
  • Excitation energy: $$\omega_{\text{sTDA}} = \sqrt{ \sum_{\mu<\nu} \frac{S_{\mu\nu}^2}{R_{\mu\nu}^6} }$$
  • Minimal basis sets, point charge approximations
  • Ignores exchange, correlation, and orbital relaxation

Best For

Very large systems (1,000+ atoms) like proteins, nanomaterials, and high-throughput screening.

Which Method Should You Use?

What's your system size?

• Small molecule (< 100 atoms)
• Medium molecule (100-500 atoms)
• Large system (> 500 atoms)

Small molecule?

→ Use TDDFT with hybrid functional (e.g., B3LYP)
→ If charge-transfer states: switch to TDA

Medium molecule?

→ TDA is ideal (avoids TDDFT errors, feasible cost)
→ Avoid full TDDFT (too expensive)

Large system?

→ sTDA is the only practical choice
→ TDDFT/TDA would take months; sTDA takes hours

Key Takeaways

Understanding these methods helps you choose the right tool for your computational chemistry needs. Remember:

TDDFT

Gold standard for small molecules but struggles with charge-transfer states

TDA

Fixes TDDFT's flaws for medium systems - often more accurate than full TDDFT

sTDA

Ultra-fast for huge systems but sacrifices quantum accuracy for speed
