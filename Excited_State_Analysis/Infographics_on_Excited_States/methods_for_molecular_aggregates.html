  • FOB-SH: Fragment-based; simulates up to 10‚Åµ atoms

‚úÖ Conclusion & Recommendations

Key Takeaways

  1. No single method fits all ‚Äî choose based on system size, process, and accuracy.
  2. Exciton models are powerful but must include vibronic and CT effects.
  3. Conical intersections (intra- and intermolecular) control nonradiative decay.
  4. Multiconfigurational methods (CASPT2) are essential for SF and TTA.
  5. New dynamics methods (FOB-SH, ML) enable large-scale simulations beyond perturbative limits.
  6. Integration of theory and experiment drives innovation in OLEDs, OSCs, and phosphors.

For Students & Researchers

  • Start with TDDFT + dimer models for screening.
  • Use exciton models for optical properties and energy transfer.
  • Apply CDFT for CT states; CASPT2 for SF/TTA.
  • Leverage ML tools and libraries like fromage (Rivera et al.) and SHARC.
  • Validate simulations with experimental spectra and device data.
